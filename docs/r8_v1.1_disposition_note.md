## §1 Context

R8 v1.0 contained a conceptual conflation between two distinct compression layers:

| Compression layer | Parameter (main) | Produces `.tern-model`? | Observable via R7-A teacher-forcing PPL? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Weight compression | `threshold` (`src/terncore/convert.py TernaryInferenceEngine.convert`) | yes | yes — directly |
| KV cache compression | `b_mse` (`tools/tern_infer.py IncrementalTQCompressor` → `TurboQuantConfig`) | no — runtime only | no — single-window forward pass doesn't iteratively accumulate cache across tokens the way autoregressive generation does |

R8 v1.0 §4 step 1 cited `b_mse` as the sweep parameter while describing a procedure that requires a `.tern-model`-producing weight compressor (which on `main` is controlled by `threshold`, not `b_mse`). The TinyLlama anchor referenced (`b_mse=3`, PPL 7.82→8.14, 4.1% delta, per `benchmarks/tq_bench_results.json` 2026-03-30) was measured against runtime KV-cache compression with an autoregressive harness — NOT R7-A teacher-forcing methodology.

The defect was annotated inline at R8 v1.0 §4 step 1 in commit `d74b093` with three resolution options proposed: (a) amend §3 to sweep `threshold`, (b) introduce a new `b_mse`-parameterised weight-compression entry point on `main`, or (c) split the spec into two diagnostics.

## §3 Rationale

### §3.1 Eagle bracket alignment

Three of four Eagle brackets from the R4-C era empirical work require KV-cache-compression empirical evidence:

- **Bracket 1** — Heterogeneous attention KV cache adapter
- **Bracket 2** — Seeded regenerable shared projection matrices
- **Bracket 3** — Constant per-token encode cost at scale (corroborated by both compression-layer frontiers)

Bracket 4 / P157 (`.tern-model` native bench harness) is methodology infrastructure consumed by both diagnostics.

Option (a) — threshold-only sweep — would orphan the KV-cache empirical work entirely, severing R8's connection to three of the four Eagle brackets. Option (c) preserves and extends KV-cache empirical work as a distinct, methodologically defensible diagnostic (R12) while making the weight-compression frontier (R8 v1.1) immediately executable.

### §3.2 Empirical asset preservation

The existing TinyLlama-1.1B `b_mse=3` measurement (`benchmarks/tq_bench_results.json`, 2026-03-30) is real on-disk empirical data: PPL 7.82→8.14, 4.1% delta, 5.3× KV memory compression. Under option (c) this anchor lives legitimately under R12 as a KV-cache PPL calibration point (subject to R7-B-conformance re-baselining or formal grandfathering). Under options (a) and (b) the anchor is lost or distorted by being forced into a methodology that doesn't measure what it measured.

### §3.3 Methodology cleanness

The two compression layers are genuinely independent — they control different things, operate at different lifecycle phases (build-time vs runtime), and produce different measurable effects. Conflating them was the original v1.0 error; the corrective should be clean separation, not a lesser conflation that picks one and orphans the other.

Each diagnostic under option (c) is internally consistent: R8 v1.1 measures weight-compression effects via a methodology (R7-A teacher-forcing) that exercises them; R12 measures KV-cache compression effects via a methodology (R7-B autoregressive, to be drafted) that exercises them.
